Yes. No account, no email, no paywall. RacketFit is open source and runs entirely in your browser.

No. The grip question is optional. Leave it blank and we suggest the most common size with instructions to measure yourself.

Guide

How the matching works

Picking a racket by brand is a trap. What matters is the spec sheet: a heavier, head-light, flexible 98 plays nothing like a light, stiff, head-heavy 105. RacketFit turns how you play into the numbers you should be shopping for.

1. Your answers become a target spec

Each survey answer nudges a baseline up or down. A beginner gets a bigger head and lighter weight; an arm-sensitive player gets a lower stiffness, weighted more heavily. Nothing is hidden, every adjustment is a small, readable number.

2. Every racket is scored

For each spec, a racket scores 1.0 at your ideal and fades toward 0 as it drifts outside your comfortable range. String pattern is matched separately. Weighted together, that becomes a 0 to 100 match.

3. String, tension, and grip

Strings are scored on power, control, spin, comfort, durability, and feel against what your game needs. Tension is tuned inside the string's normal range: lower for power and comfort, higher for control. Grip size comes from your hand measurement, snapped to a standard size.

The cheat sheet

  • Bigger head = more power and forgiveness. Smaller = more control.
  • Heavier = more stability and plow-through, harder to swing fast.
  • Head-light = maneuverable. Head-heavy = more mass behind the ball.
  • Lower RA (stiffness) = more flex and comfort. Higher = more power, harsher.
  • Open pattern (16x19) = more spin. Dense (18x20) = more control.
  • Lower tension = more power. Higher tension = more control.
